Introduction: what is Agile and Scrum?

Agile is a flexible methodology that includes a number of different methods to process management. One of the most common variants - Scrum.

The whole point of the Scrum methodology

Artifacts in Scrum:

  • product backlog
  • sprint backlog
  • increment

Product backlog

It is led by the owner or product manager. This is an ever-changing list of functionalities, requirements, improvements and fixes from which the tasks for the backup sprint are compiled.

Backlog sprint

Increment

Some teams prefer to produce something for their customers at the end of each sprint. For them, the word «done» means «delivered».

Here are the main meetings:

  • Backlog Organization
    • Keeps the list, changes its priorities and keeps it up to date based on information from users and the development team

    Here are the main meetings:

  • Sprint Planning
    • At this meeting, a team of developers under the guidance of the Scrum Master plans the work

    Here are the main meetings:

  • Daily meeting
    1. What did I do yesterday?
    2. What do I plan to do today?
    3. Can I be prevented by something?

    Here are the main meetings:

  • Overview of sprint results

    • The development team presents the completed work tasks from the backlog to stakeholders and colleagues to gather feedback.

    Here are the main meetings:

  • Sprint Retrospective

    • The goal of the retrospective is to create the conditions so that the team can pay attention to everything that has been achieved and what needs to be improved next time, and not dwell on failures.

    Roles in Scrum:

  • Scrum Product Owner

    • Their task is to understand the requirements of business, customer and market.

    Roles in Scrum:

  • Scrum master

    • Scrum masters monitor the application of Scrum principles in their commands.

    Roles in Scrum:

  • Scrum Development Team

    • The Scrum team is responsible for all the work. They are experts in the principles of balanced development.
